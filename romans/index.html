Romans · Book Introduction
An Introduction to the Book of

Romans

The Gospel That Changed the World

If you could only keep one book of the Bible besides the Gospels, which would it be? For many Christians throughout history, the answer has been Romans.

Romans has transformed lives, launched revivals, shaped theology, and clarified the gospel for countless believers. It was instrumental in the conversions of men such as Augustine, Martin Luther, and John Wesley.

Romans is Paul's fullest explanation of:

  • Who God is
  • What sin is
  • Why Jesus came
  • How salvation works
  • What faith means
  • How Christians should live
  • God's plan for Israel and the nations

If the Bible were a mountain range, Romans would be one of its highest peaks.

Author: The Apostle Paul

Romans identifies its author immediately (1:1). God uniquely prepared him to explain how the gospel fulfills God's promises to Israel while opening salvation to the entire world.

Date & Setting

A.D. 56–58, during Paul's third missionary journey. Written from Corinth, while Paul was preparing to carry a financial gift to believers in Jerusalem.

Paul had never visited this church. Unlike most of his letters, Romans was not written to solve a crisis. Paul writes to explain the gospel thoroughly and prepare for future ministry.

The Major Theme

The Righteousness of God Revealed

Romans is fundamentally about how sinful people can be made right with a holy God.

Keywords

  • Righteousness
  • Justified
  • Faith
  • Grace
  • Sin
  • Law

The central question is: How can God be just and still save sinners?

Key Verse

Romans 1:16–17
"For I am not ashamed of the gospel, because it is the power of God for salvation to everyone who believes, first to the Jew, and also to the Greek. For in it the righteousness of God is revealed from faith to faith…" Romans 1:16–17

These verses function like the thesis statement of the entire book. Everything that follows expands these two verses.

Why Romans Still Matters

Our culture asks:

Who am I?
What is wrong with the world?
Is there truth?
How can I be accepted?
What happens after death?

Romans answers all of them. The book begins with humanity's deepest problem and ends with God's glorious solution.

It teaches that the greatest need of every person is not self-improvement, political reform, education, wealth, or success.

The greatest need is reconciliation with God through Jesus Christ.
